## How telcos buy in 2026

U.S. carriers invest in platforms that either **defend margins (OpEx compression)** or **enable new B2B revenue (Techco pivot)**. Boards tend to prioritize projects that cut cost, consolidate vendors, or prevent SLA/regulatory penalties.

**Wall Street lens:** ~35% EBITDA margins · flat ARPU · post-5G CapEx peak · heavy debt.

## Multi-transport defensive alliance

- **May 2026:** AT&T, Verizon, T-Mobile — agreement in principle for **tri-carrier D2D JV** (pooled spectrum, unified spec).
- **Catalyst:** Starlink Mobile **9M+ subs** — orbital broadband on standard phones.
- **T-Mobile nuance:** T-Satellite (Starlink partnership) live, but satellite = **0.0002%** of usage — telemetry must be negligible OpEx.
- **Demo pivot:** D2D changes the long game; **iPhone 18 launch weekend** is still when subscribers choose you or leave.

## Compliance sound bites

- **FCC KYC:** up to **$2,500 per call** for structural violations; **4-year** post-termination retention.
- **T-Mobile 10DLC:** Tier 1 **$2K** (phishing/smishing) up to **$10K** per violation for evasion.
- Tie to launch: high activation volume = high KYC/SMS audit surface — indexed once, query for legal + ops.
